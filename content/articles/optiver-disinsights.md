My initial opinion on quantitative finance is similar to that of your friend's, probably, that it is the most challenging sector requiring the brightest and sometimes weirdest minds, using math and tech stuff to exploit “market inefficiencies”, which are fancy words for people making suboptimal decisions - the more suboptimal the better. Most importantly, it pays awesome sauce. Recruitment asks you to solve problems that are high school level, and by high school level, I mean the IMO. Some ask you to do mental math and F1 training as well. Especially Optiver with their *80 in 8* and *Zap-N*, the firm whose event - Optiver Insights Day - I’m about to attend. 

Reading the invitation makes me feel like a five-year-old on his first Take Your Son to Work Day. Years (1.5) of being in the dark, finally, I can get to know what the adults do.

My impression after the whole thing is that they don’t share much. 

Nothing new was presented that day, frankly. Optiver regurgitated things that were already on their website. The advice was along the lines of “Stay humble, ask questions, study well in college, take risks, focus on behaviors” (by the way, the person advising that wrote about the time he went on a waterslide and connected that to risk - there’s nothing risky about a waterslide. Risk means you may lose something; after you’ve paid for the tickets, waterslides are at best risk-free and most of the time straight up arbitrage). When asked how Optiver integrates technology into trading, one of them said: “You know, AI, right, using it ermmm, you know, you have to use it carefully. I personally don’t know a lot of AI, so I uhh, I’m gonna have to learn” (something along those lines). Perhaps the traders said some more interesting stuff during the networking, but any individual with an intact brain and mild interest would be able to infer all the information given in the entire duration of the 4-hour event. 

“Why the dread, though?” I’d ask myself. “You attended an exclusive event, and you did meet some nice people”

“Yknow, it’s ‘exclusive’, so I was hoping for more”

“As in…”

“More information, more human-to-human interaction, not a smoothly run event beginning to end where traders don’t seem like they want to be there”

“Ehhhh, I mean, they’re traders. If you were a trader, you also wouldn’t want to be there, would you?”

“True.”

“Also, they did seem genuine during the panel discussion.”

“You think?”

“I don’t think that much stuttering can happen if there were a premade script.”

“Okay, maybe, but so what?”

“What if they have said all there is?”

“?”

“What if there genuinely is only that, and you’ve known all that you need to know to do something?”

I flinched. 

“Whoa, why the reaction? Heh, could it be that you’re afraid of…?”

I had a girl I really liked in middle school, let’s call her Like, pretty sure that’s a literal translation of her name. I liked her very much. How her eyes were a bit smaller and sleeker. How her fragrant hair softened the air as she walked by. How hard she worked. How she was still herself after all the pressure from her typical Asian mother. How her laugh seemed to contain the joy of a thousand universes. She was popular, too. Way too popular. Way out of my league in every aspect imaginable. 

And so I kept my feelings hidden. All the way through middle school and high school. I would catch glimpses of her and quickly turn away. Always. A second longer and they would be stuck there, permanently. And gone she was. To America. 

I couldn’t have known more than I did about her. I had way more than enough “data” to know that I like her. And yet I was frozen in place for years.

My feelings were big. But my fears were bigger. They always have been. I have always been like this. Afraid to act. To do what I truly want or need to do. To tell her with utmost sincerity, how much I like her. To put myself out there in the world against people more qualified and more driven for a chance. What if I fail? Nothing would happen, in both cases. But still.

Before this, I had hoped that what I felt was original. Of course it wasn’t. Shakespeare wrote Hamlet (about a guy arguably more noble than me), and I am willing to bet my life on the fact that at least a dozen modern romcom manga have indecisive main characters (most, again, arguably more noble than me, with the sole exception of Kazuya from Rent-a-Girlfriend). But that isn’t as necessary as the bet I need to make, that I won’t still be like these characters in the future. I wasn’t able to take the first step last time. Will I be able to take the first step this time?

Heheheh, who knows…
