The idea of creating a website has been lingering at the back of my mind for a while now. Today, after I have gathered enough anger and boredom at myself and the world around me, this little corner of mine in this vast sea of information is created. 

If you had the link to this website, that probably means you know who I am, or at the very least, my name, my university and my major. To some, or to most, actually, that would be more than enough information to gauge a person's "worthiness". Nevertheless, I'm going to tell you what isn't already on my LinkedIn profile.

I come from a fairly typical lower-middle class family in Hanoi, Vietnam. My passion in life is undoubtedly Mathematics, namely in Abstract Algebra. There are two distinct interactions that I still remember to this day: one where I read Dummit & Foote's first few chapters on the basics and examples of Group Theory, and one where I entered the office of the man Phung Ho Hai himself and witnessed the neatly presented yet incomprehensible combinations of symbols that I back then believed to be "as close to witchcraft as you can get". Both times I leave with nothing but a sense of admiration and awe and ecstasy akin to that of a kid that pedals for the first time. 

Such passion has led me to pursue the major of Mathematical Sciences in Nanyang Technological University. My dear reader, are you happy for me after hearing this? Well curb your satisfaction because you are in for a major letdown, just as I was. The Mathematics students of the School of Physical and Mathematical Sciences is obsessed with one thing and one thing only: being a *quant*. In case you live under a rock, a *quant* refers to a person who works in *quantitative finance*, where you abuse the work of gods to help you make money (using mathematics and programming to calculate risks, price options, trade stocks, "utilize market inefficiencies", ...). I firmly believe that this as well as plenty of other jobs in the finance sector brings people on a path to surefire mental corruption, because you are effectively contributing nothing while earning everything. It's the kind of job I dread most, and yet, the earnings are too good for me to not try and shake the devil's hands for once.

And now here I am, stranded in the middle of the road. People around me are heading straight for their goals, albeit with varying paces, while I'm still struggling to put out anything of value. Young and hot-headed as I am, I can't let go (holy GD reference) of either my love or my money. How will I be able to feed myself without a proper job here in this capitalistic hellhole, and if I can't pursue beauty, what am I feeding myself for? Hence, the most plausible solution I can come up with is to pursue both at once. And this place here serves as a diary of some sort to record my efforts, my struggles, my joy, my pain, my anger, my everything as I try, perhaps in vain, to juggle two dumbbells at once. 

Whenever I learn something new in abstract algebra or in quantitative finance, I will create an article of that topic in here, all written in my own words and my own understanding of the topic. Additionally, I will sneak in my own contemplations and feelings towards these new concepts as much as possible. Sometimes I'll feel goofy, sometimes I'll feel sad, sometimes I'll feel nothing. Additionally, I'll try to keep a weekly update, similar to this article right here, where I'll cover things that happened and things that I've learnt. Mostly things I've learnt, since about nothing happens to me every week here.

Today will not be the day I give up, tomorrow I am not so sure.
